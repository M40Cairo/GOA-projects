Volleyball Rules

  • 1. Scoring:Matches are typically played as the best of 5 sets. Each set is played to 25 points, with a two-point advantage required to win the set. If the match reaches a fifth set (if necessary), it is played to 15 points, with the same two-point advantage requirement.
  • 2. Teams: Each team consists of six players on the court at a time: three front-row players and three back-row players. Teams can make unlimited substitutions during stoppages in play.
  • 3. Playing the Ball: Each rally begins with a serve, where one team serves the ball over the net to the opposing team. After the serve, teams have three contacts (touches) to return the ball over the net: Typically, the first contact is a pass (or bump), the second is a set, and the third is an attack (or spike). Players cannot touch the ball consecutively, except for blocking a shot at the net.
  • 4. Scoring Points: Points are awarded when the ball lands in the opponent's court or if the opponent commits a fault. Common faults include: A ball landing out of bounds. A player making contact with the net during play. A player touching the ball four times before returning it to the opponent's court. A player committing a double hit (two successive contacts by the same player). A player committing a lift (holding or catching the ball).
  • 5. Rotations: Players rotate positions on the court after winning a rally and earning the right to serve. Players must rotate in a clockwise direction when looking from above.
  • 6. Net Play: Players can reach over the net to block a shot, but they cannot interfere with the opponent's play until after the opponent's attack has crossed the net. Players are not allowed to touch the net during play.
  • 7. Libero: Each team may designate one player as the libero, who wears a different color jersey. The libero is a defensive specialist who cannot serve, attack the ball above the net, or attempt to block.
